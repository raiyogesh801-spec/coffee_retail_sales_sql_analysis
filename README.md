# coffee_retail_sales_sql_analysis
Business focused SQL analysis of coffee retail sales data to identify revenue trends, top products, and high potential markets.
## Overview

This project analyzes coffee shop sales data using SQL to uncover insights about revenue trends, product performance, customer behavior, and market potential.

The analysis simulates real-world business decision-making for a coffee retail company inspired by the **Monday Coffee** business model.

---

## Objective

The goal of this project is to demonstrate how SQL can be used to solve real business problems by:

• Identifying high-revenue cities
• Discovering top-selling products
• Analyzing customer purchasing behavior
• Tracking monthly sales trends
• Evaluating market expansion opportunities

---

## Key Business Questions

1. How many people in each city are estimated to consume coffee?
2. What is the total revenue generated in the last quarter of 2023?
3. Which products generate the highest number of orders?
4. What is the average sales amount per customer in each city?
5. How many unique coffee customers exist in each city?
6. What are the top 3 best-selling products by city?
7. How does customer spending compare with estimated city rent?
8. What are the monthly sales growth trends?
9. Which cities show the highest market potential?
10. What percentage of total revenue comes from each product?

---

## Key Insights

• **Pune and Delhi generate the highest revenue**, indicating strong market demand.
• A small number of products contribute to a large portion of total sales.
• Customer spending varies significantly across cities.
• Monthly sales analysis reveals fluctuations in demand and potential seasonal trends.

---

## Business Recommendations

• Expand operations in high-revenue cities such as **Pune, Delhi, and Jaipur**.
• Focus marketing efforts on **top-performing coffee products**.
• Introduce premium product strategies in cities with higher customer spending.
• Target cities with large estimated coffee consumer populations for future expansion.
