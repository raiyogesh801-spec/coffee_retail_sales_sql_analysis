# coffee_retail_sales_sql_analysis
Business focused SQL analysis of coffee retail sales data to identify revenue trends, top products, and high potential markets.
# Coffee Retail Sales Intelligence Analysis (SQL)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes coffee shop sales data using SQL to uncover key business insights related to customer behavior, product performance, revenue trends, and city-level market opportunities.

The case study is inspired by **Monday Coffee**, a community-focused specialty coffee brand founded in Chicago in 2020 by entrepreneurs Amanda Christine Harth and Felton Kizer. The company focuses on building community through coffee culture and experiential café spaces. ([Daily Coffee News by Roast Magazine][1])

Using SQL, this project transforms raw sales data into actionable insights that could support business decisions such as market expansion, product strategy, and customer targeting.

---

# Project Objective

The goal of this analysis is to demonstrate how SQL can be used to solve real-world business problems in a retail coffee environment.

The project focuses on:

• Identifying high-performing cities based on revenue
• Understanding customer purchasing behavior
• Discovering the most popular coffee products
• Analyzing sales growth trends over time
• Evaluating potential markets for future expansion

The ultimate objective is to convert raw transactional data into **data driven business recommendations**.

---

# Key Business Questions

This project answers several analytical questions including:

1. How many people in each city are estimated to consume coffee based on population data?
2. What is the total revenue generated from coffee sales in the last quarter of 2023?
3. Which coffee products generate the highest number of orders?
4. What is the average sales amount per customer in each city?
5. How many unique coffee customers exist in each city?
6. What are the top 3 best-selling products in each city?
7. How does customer spending compare with estimated city rent?
8. How has monthly sales performance changed across cities?
9. Which cities show the highest potential for business expansion?
10. What percentage of total revenue is contributed by each product?

These questions simulate the type of analysis a data analyst would perform to support **strategic decision-making in a retail coffee business**.

---

# Key Insights from the Analysis

### High Revenue Cities

Cities such as **Pune and Delhi** generated the highest total revenue, indicating strong coffee demand and high market potential.

### Large Coffee Consumer Markets

Cities with larger populations also show a higher estimated number of coffee consumers, suggesting significant untapped market opportunities.

### Product Demand Concentration

A small group of coffee products contributes to the majority of sales, indicating clear customer preferences and strong demand patterns.

### Sales Growth Trends

Monthly sales analysis revealed fluctuations in revenue, which helps identify peak sales periods and seasonal demand patterns.

### Customer Spending Behavior

Average spending per customer varies significantly across cities, highlighting differences in customer purchasing power and consumption habits.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on the insights generated from the analysis, the following recommendations can be made:

### 1. Expand Operations in High-Potential Cities

Cities such as **Pune, Delhi, and Jaipur** demonstrate strong revenue potential combined with relatively manageable operating costs.

### 2. Focus on Top Selling Products

High-performing products should be prioritized in marketing campaigns, promotions, and inventory planning.

### 3. Introduce Premium Product Strategies

Cities with higher average spending per customer may support premium coffee offerings and specialty menu items.

### 4. Target Cities with Large Coffee Consumer Bases

Cities with large populations and high estimated coffee consumption represent strong candidates for future store expansion.
